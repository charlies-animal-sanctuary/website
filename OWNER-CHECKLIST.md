# Charlie's Animal Sanctuary — what the website needs from you

The site is already built and working — it's just wearing obviously-fake placeholder
content. Everything on this list swaps a placeholder for the real thing. **Nothing
needs to be polished** — rough notes and camera-roll photos are exactly right. You'll
get to see and approve everything before it goes live.

---

## 1. Two links and one key (~15 minutes)

- [ ] **Web3Forms key** — this is what makes the site's forms (adoption applications,
  surrender requests, contact messages) arrive in your email inbox.
  1. Go to **web3forms.com**
  2. Type in the email address where you want those messages to arrive, and click
     **Create Access Key**
  3. They'll email you an "Access Key" — **forward that email to Faraaz** (don't post
     the key anywhere public)
  That's the whole thing. No password, no real account, free.

- [ ] **PayPal donation link** — the link people should land on when they hit "Donate."
  From PayPal, copy your donation/PayPal.me link and send it over.

- [ ] **YouTube video** — which video should be the "Watch our story" feature on the
  homepage? Just send the link to the one you want.

*(Your Instagram is already wired up — nothing needed there.)*

## 2. Your words — the most important part

Open one doc (Google Doc, Notes app, anything) and just write like you're texting a
friend. Bullet points are fine. Typos are fine. It gets shaped into website copy, and
**you approve every word before it's published.**

- [ ] **Your story** (for the About page): how the rescue started, what you do day to
  day, and where you want it to go (the farm sanctuary dream).
- [ ] **Charlie** — the sanctuary is named after your horse, and the website should
  tell that story. Who is/was Charlie? This might be the heart of the About page.
- [ ] **Each pet's story** (for the Our Family pages): Ash, Midna, and anyone else —
  how they came to you, one funny or sweet detail, anything quirky. 2–5 sentences
  each is plenty.
- [ ] **How surrendering works** (for the Intake page): what should someone know or
  expect when they need to give up an animal or found a stray? What do you want to
  ask them?
- [ ] **What happens after someone applies to adopt**: the steps in your words (meet
  and greet? home check? typical fee, and what it covers?).
- [ ] **The one-liner**: if the homepage could say one sentence about the sanctuary,
  what would it be? (Give a few tries — or skip it, and options will be drafted from
  your story for you to pick from.)
- [ ] **Brag a little**: how long you've been rescuing, roughly how many animals
  you've placed, anything you're proud of. Real numbers make donors trust the site.

---

## 3. Photos — send lots, we'll pick together

Easiest way: dump everything into a shared album or folder (Google Photos / Drive /
whatever you use). Send **originals** — no cropping, no filters, the biggest files
you have.

- [ ] **Hero photo candidates** — the big photo at the top of the homepage. Send
  several options. Ideas, best first:
  - **Charlie** — the namesake front and center would be perfect and tells the story
    in one image
  - You with an animal, or an animal looking straight at the camera in nice light
  - A warm everyday moment — feeding time, a cat pile in a sunbeam
  - Sideways/landscape photos work best for this spot
- [ ] **A photo of you** (with animals is even better) — for the About page.
- [ ] **Each Our Family pet**: one clear portrait + a few extras each. Tall/vertical
  photos crop nicest on the animal cards. (Should Charlie get his own page here too?
  If yes — photos + his story.)
- [ ] **Your 6–9 favourite Instagram photos** — for the photo strip on the homepage.
  If you can, include the link to each post (open the post → ⋯ or share → Copy link)
  so each photo clicks through to the real post. You'll be able to change these
  yourself later in the site's admin, the same place you'll manage the animals.
- [ ] **Anything else you love** — extras and outtakes welcome; galleries can absorb
  a lot.
